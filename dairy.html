Dairy overview

Dairy offers regular income when routine and feed planning stay strong

Why dairy

Milk offers daily or frequent cash flow, supports family nutrition, and integrates well with fodder, manure, and crop systems.

Core success factors

Breed suitability, feed availability, clean housing, calm milking routine, and disease prevention shape long-term results.

Best use

Dairy works well for households that can maintain consistent daily care and market milk reliably.

🐄

Breeds

Choose breeds that match climate, feed, and management capacity

Indigenous breeds

Local breeds can offer better heat tolerance and adaptability, especially where management resources are limited.

Crossbred animals

Crossbreds may give stronger milk performance but usually need better feeding, comfort, and health support.

Breed selection rule

Always choose animals based on local support, disease history, feed resources, and milk marketing plan.

🌾

Feeding basics

Balanced feeding supports milk yield, body condition, and fertility

Fodder base

Green fodder, dry roughage, and concentrates should be balanced according to milk stage and body condition.

Minerals & water

Mineral mixture and uninterrupted clean water are essential for milk production and overall health.

Feed discipline

Regular timing and gradual feed changes reduce digestive stress and production fluctuation.

🏠

Housing

Clean sheds protect comfort, udder health, and labour efficiency

Dry flooring

Dry floors and proper drainage help control hoof trouble, smell, and hygiene problems.

Ventilation

Fresh air lowers heat stress and makes routine work easier during warm conditions.

Rest area

Comfortable resting space helps maintain productivity and reduces avoidable stress.

🥛

Milking management

A calm, hygienic milking routine protects quality and consistency

Routine

Milk at regular times and avoid sudden disruptions that can affect let-down and yield.

Clean udder care

Clean hands, teats, and utensils help reduce mastitis risk and improve milk quality.

Record output

Milk records help track feed response, health changes, and animal performance.

🩺

Health

Observation and prevention are better than delayed treatment

Watch daily

Appetite, cud chewing, dung, temperature stress, udder changes, and lameness are worth checking every day.

Vaccination & deworming

Follow local veterinary schedules instead of random medicine use.

Early action

Call for help early if fever, udder swelling, abortion, breathing trouble, or sudden weakness appears.

🏛️

Schemes

Use schemes, credit, and training as structured support

Dairy unit support

Project-linked support may cover animal purchase, shed development, and dairy-related infrastructure.

Bank credit

Loans may help with working capital, fodder planning, and equipment needs.

Training

Field training can improve milking hygiene, ration planning, and day-to-day management.

Beginner tips

Start with a routine you can sustain daily

  • Choose healthy animals from trusted sources.
  • Secure fodder and water before buying animals.
  • Maintain one notebook for milk, feed, breeding, and treatment.
  • Keep the shed dry and calm during milking hours.
  • Expand only after the first unit becomes stable.
❓

FAQ

Simple dairy questions, quickly answered

Is dairy suitable for beginners?

Yes, if the family can maintain regular feeding, clean housing, and reliable daily care.

What is the biggest beginner mistake?

Starting animals before securing fodder, clean water, and a milk marketing plan often creates avoidable stress.

Why are records important?

They help connect milk changes with feed, health, breeding, and management decisions.
