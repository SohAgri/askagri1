Poultry & Livestock Farming Guide

Poultry and livestock farming are important parts of agriculture in India. They help farmers generate regular income, improve family nutrition through eggs, milk, and meat, and create local employment in rural areas.

🐔 Poultry Farming

1) Introduction to Poultry Farming

Poultry farming means rearing birds such as chickens, ducks, and quails for eggs and meat. It is a fast-return farming activity that can be started on small land. Poultry supports household income, women-led enterprises, and year-round food supply.

2) Types of Poultry Birds

🐤 Broiler

Purpose: Meat

Broilers are fast-growing birds raised mainly for meat, usually sold in 5-7 weeks.

  • Management tip: Keep brooder temperature proper in early weeks and provide protein-rich feed.

🥚 Layer

Purpose: Eggs

Layers are specialized birds for egg production with long laying cycles.

  • Management tip: Give calcium-rich layer feed and maintain regular lighting schedule.

🐓 Country Chicken / Desi Poultry

Purpose: Meat + Eggs

Hardy indigenous birds suitable for backyard and semi-intensive systems.

  • Management tip: Provide night shelter, local grains, and regular vaccination.

🦆 Duck

Purpose: Eggs + Meat

Ducks perform well in wet areas and can be integrated with paddy fields.

  • Management tip: Ensure clean water access and dry resting area.

🐥 Quail

Purpose: Eggs + Meat

Quails need less space and mature quickly, useful for small entrepreneurs.

  • Management tip: Use fine balanced feed and avoid sudden noise/stress.

3) Poultry Housing and Management

  • Poultry shed design: Elevated, dry floor with easy cleaning and separate brooding/growing areas.
  • Ventilation: Fresh air reduces ammonia build-up and disease risk.
  • Lighting: Controlled lighting supports feeding and egg production.
  • Litter management: Keep litter dry, loose, and replace wet patches quickly.
  • Sanitation: Clean feeders, drinkers, and entry area regularly with disinfectant.

4) Poultry Feed and Nutrition

  • Starter feed: High protein feed for chicks (first 0-3 weeks).
  • Grower feed: Balanced feed for growth stage.
  • Layer feed: Calcium and minerals for shell quality and egg output.
  • Clean drinking water: Continuous fresh water is essential in all seasons.
  • Mineral and vitamin supplements: Useful during stress, vaccination, and seasonal changes.

5) Poultry Diseases

Ranikhet Disease (Newcastle)

  • Symptoms: Sneezing, neck twisting, low egg output, sudden death.
  • Causes: Viral infection spread by birds, people, and equipment.
  • Prevention: Timely vaccination, strict biosecurity, isolate sick birds.

Coccidiosis

  • Symptoms: Bloody droppings, weakness, poor growth.
  • Causes: Protozoa in wet litter and contaminated sheds.
  • Prevention: Keep litter dry, use coccidiostats as advised.

Fowl Pox

  • Symptoms: Wart-like lesions on comb/wattle, breathing trouble (wet form).
  • Causes: Viral spread through mosquitoes and wounds.
  • Prevention: Vaccinate at right age and control mosquitoes.

CRD (Chronic Respiratory Disease)

  • Symptoms: Coughing, nasal discharge, reduced feed intake.
  • Causes: Mycoplasma infection, poor ventilation, stress.
  • Prevention: Good airflow, clean housing, buy healthy chicks.

6) Poultry Farming Tips

  • ✅ Maintain cleanliness in shed and equipment.
  • ✅ Provide balanced feed as per bird age.
  • ✅ Avoid overcrowding in poultry house.
  • ✅ Vaccinate birds on time.
  • ✅ Observe birds daily for early disease signs.

🐄 Livestock Farming

1) Introduction to Livestock

Livestock farming includes rearing cattle, buffalo, goats, sheep, and pigs for milk, meat, manure, and income. It strengthens rural livelihoods, supports mixed farming, and provides financial stability through daily or periodic returns.

2) Major Livestock Animals

🐄 Cow

Purpose: Milk (and manure)

Cows are key dairy animals and can support biogas and organic farming systems.

  • Basic care tip: Provide clean shed, regular milking, and balanced ration.

🐃 Buffalo

Purpose: Milk + Meat

Buffalo milk has higher fat and good market demand in many Indian states.

  • Basic care tip: Provide wallowing/cooling in hot weather and mineral mixture.

🐐 Goat

Purpose: Meat + Milk

Goats are suitable for smallholders due to low initial investment and quick reproduction.

  • Basic care tip: Deworm regularly and keep raised, dry housing.

🐑 Sheep

Purpose: Meat + Wool

Sheep are useful in dryland systems and can graze on open pasture.

  • Basic care tip: Ensure shade, clean water, and vaccination against local diseases.

🐖 Pig

Purpose: Meat

Pig farming gives quick weight gain and good feed conversion for meat production.

  • Basic care tip: Maintain hygienic pens and strictly follow vaccination schedule.

3) Housing and Shelter

  • Proper shed construction: Strong, easy-to-clean, and enough space per animal.
  • Ventilation: Natural airflow reduces heat stress and disease.
  • Drainage: Sloped floor and drainage channel prevent water stagnation.
  • Extreme weather protection: Provide shade in summer and dry bedding in winter/rain.

4) Feed and Fodder

  • Green fodder: Napier, maize, sorghum, and legume fodders for nutrients.
  • Dry fodder: Hay and straw for roughage and rumen health.
  • Concentrate feed: Oil cakes, grains, bran for higher production.
  • Mineral mixture: Essential to prevent deficiency and improve fertility.
  • Clean drinking water: Required throughout the day for milk and growth.

5) Common Livestock Diseases

Foot and Mouth Disease (FMD)

  • Symptoms: Fever, mouth ulcers, excessive salivation, foot lesions.
  • Causes: Highly contagious viral disease.
  • Prevention: Regular vaccination and movement control.

Mastitis

  • Symptoms: Swollen udder, pain, flakes or watery milk.
  • Causes: Bacterial infection due to poor milking hygiene.
  • Prevention: Clean milking routine and teat dipping.

Black Quarter (BQ)

  • Symptoms: Fever, painful muscle swelling, sudden death in young cattle.
  • Causes: Clostridial bacterial infection from contaminated soil.
  • Prevention: Annual vaccination and proper carcass disposal.

Hemorrhagic Septicemia (HS)

  • Symptoms: High fever, throat swelling, breathing difficulty.
  • Causes: Bacterial infection common during monsoon stress.
  • Prevention: Pre-monsoon vaccination and stress reduction.

6) Livestock Farming Tips

  • ✅ Choose healthy and active animals from trusted source.
  • ✅ Provide clean and comfortable shelter.
  • ✅ Follow a fixed feeding schedule.
  • ✅ Maintain regular vaccination and deworming.
  • ✅ Consult a veterinarian quickly when symptoms appear.
